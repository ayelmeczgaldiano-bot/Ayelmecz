Core Foundation

Who We Are

The Sisters of Mary Schools (SMS) were founded as part of the mission of the Religious Congregation of the Sisters of Mary, established by Venerable Aloysius Schwartz on August 15, 1964, in Busan, South Korea. The primary goal of these schools is to provide free, high-quality education to children from impoverished families. The initiative expanded to the Philippines in 1985, where the Sisters of Mary established four schools: Biga Girlstown, Adlas Boystown, Talisay Girlstown, and Minglanilla Boystown. These institutions, known as Boystowns and Girlstowns, function as boarding schools that offer not only education but also comprehensive care, including nutrition, lodging, and medical services, all at no cost to the students.

Our focus is on creating a nurturing environment where spiritual values, work ethics, discipline, and advanced vocational skills are fully integrated, enabling graduates to lift their families out of extreme poverty.

Our Inspiration

Ven. Aloysius Philip Schwartz (Father Al) ,founded the Sisters of Mary in Seoul, South Korea in 1964. Driven by deep love for orphans and the sick, his vision was simple: give children the highest quality of life and let them master a trade. Today, his spirit continues in thousands of graduates and Sisters across our international schools.
